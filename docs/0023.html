How to say to an Asian parent that you like to move out?
Having a conversation about moving out with any parent, including Asian parents, often requires careful consideration and sensitivity, as it could be a significant change for both you and your parents. Here's a step-by-step guide which might help:

1. Do Your Research: Before you discuss moving out, make sure you have a solid plan. It's necessary to consider your income, the cost of living, potential places to stay, etc. Show your parents that you've thought about this thoroughly.

2. Choose the Right Time and Place: Find a quiet, comfortable place where you could have this conversation without any interruptions. Make sure to choose a time when they are relaxed and not stressed.

3. Be Honest: Start by expressing your feelings and explain the reasons why you want to move out. This could be for your career, personal growth, or simply the experience of living independently.

4. Show Respect: While it's important to assert your independence, it's also crucial to respect their feelings and opinions. 

5. Be Prepared for Resistance: Asian parents often have a strong sense of family unity. They might worry about your safety and ability to manage yourself. Be patient and ready to address their concerns in a calm and logical manner. 

6. Show Your Gratitude: Make sure to express your gratitude for everything they've done for you. Reassure them that you're not leaving because of any failures on their part but because you want to grow as an individual.
