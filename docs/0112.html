To answer your original question, it's not necessarily that humans have become physically weaker, but our lifestyles have drastically changed over the centuries. A combination of technological advancements, environmental factors, and dietary changes have contributed to a shift from physical labor to more sedentary habits.

Today, technology provides conveniences that reduce the need for physical labor. This, coupled with dietary changes towards more processed foods and less fresh produce, can lead to poorer overall health and physical condition.

Regarding the fear of needles, it's not so much a reflection of physical weakness, but more of conditioned response or personal discomfort - some people simply have a higher sensitivity to pain or discomfort.

So, it's not a matter of being physically weaker, but rather a change in lifestyle and societal norms. It's important to remember that each era has its own challenges and adaptations. Physical strength can be improved at any time with regular exercise and a balanced diet.
