What is a fine tuned universe? Why is gravity fine-tuned?
A "fine-tuned universe" is a concept in cosmology that suggests the conditions of the universe, from physical laws to constants, are precisely set in a way that allows for the existence of life as we know it. This concept is often used in the context of the anthropic principle, which posits that the universe must be compatible with conscious life, otherwise we wouldn't be here to observe it.

Now, speaking of gravity, when we say it's "fine-tuned", it means the magnitude of the gravitational constant (G) is set to a very specific value that allows for the formation and stability of structures in the universe. This includes atoms, stars, galaxies and even life itself. 

If gravity were significantly stronger, stars would burn out too quickly and collapse into black holes, making life as we know it impossible. On the other hand, if gravity were much weaker, stars might not form at all, and the universe would just be filled with diffuse gas.

So, the notion of fine-tuning refers to this balance: the parameters of the universe, including gravity, are such that they allow for the existence and development of complex life.
