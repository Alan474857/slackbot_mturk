To answer your original question, some of the more common incurable neurodegenerative diseases that can affect individuals under the age of 20 include:

1. Batten Disease: This is a fatal, inherited disorder of the nervous system that typically begins in childhood. The early symptoms usually appear between the ages of 5 and 10 years. Life expectancy varies depending on the type or variant of Batten disease, but many affected individuals do not survive past their late teens or twenties.

2. Spinal Muscular Atrophy (SMA): This is a genetic disorder that affects the control of muscle movement due to the loss of motor neurons. Onset can occur from infancy to adulthood, but the more severe forms usually become apparent in early childhood. Life expectancy can be significantly reduced in severe forms, although with care and management survival into adolescence or later can be possible.

3. Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy (DMD): This is a severe type of muscular dystrophy that primarily affects boys, with symptoms usually appearing before age 6. DMD progressively weakens muscles, including those of the heart and lungs. Until recently, individuals with DMD did not often survive beyond their teen years, but advances in cardiac and respiratory care have allowed many to live into their early 30s and beyond.
